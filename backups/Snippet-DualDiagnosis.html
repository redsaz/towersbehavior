Dual Diagnosis - Addressing the Behavioral Challenges

Presented by The Autism Society of Colorado, the Mile High Down Syndrome Association, and Sam Towers


Providing support to an individual with Down Syndrome, Fragile X Syndrome, or another developmental disability can be challenging; however, if the person also has Autism, providing support can be even more difficult.

This course is designed for parents, professionals, and other caregivers who wish to decrease problem behavior in individuals who have Autism and another developmental disability.

The participants in this course will learn what motivates individuals in this population to use problem behavior and what can be done to decrease the problem behavior.

The following will be taught in this course:

  • The three core characteristics of autism and how they affect behavior.
  • Characteristics commonly associated with autism and how they affect behavior.
  • How your actions, including well-meaning actions, can encourage problem behavior.
  • How to change your actions so that you don't encourage problem behavior.
  • How situations in the environment can set up your child for problem behavior.
  • How to change these situations so that your child is less likely to use problem behavior.
  • How problem behavior is triggered.
  • How to reduce the number of triggers for problem behavior.
  • How to understand what your child is communicating through problem behavior.
  • How to respond to this communication so that you don't encourage further problem behavior.
  • How to take direct observational data and analyze it. This will include practice during class using video clips of problem behavior.
