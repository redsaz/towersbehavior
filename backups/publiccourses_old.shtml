In-Home Interventions for Children with Autism

Presented by The Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC


This class is designed for parents and others who wish to provide in-home educational and behavioral interventions to children with autism.

Research has demonstrated that intensive intervention for children with autism yields very strong positive results throughout the child’s entire lifetime.  Parents and in-home services providers can be an essential part of making a dramatic, lifelong difference in the lives of children with autism.  This course will provide the participants with the training necessary to provide these interventions under the guidance of a professional well skilled in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) interventions for children with autism.

This course will provide training in four areas:
•    The core characteristics of autism and related characteristics of autism.
•    Understanding why behavior occurs and what it is communicating.
•    Implementing behavior change programs.
•    Effective instruction techniques for teaching new skills to children with autism.

This class is scheduled to occur from 9:00 until 4:00 on October 28, November 4, November 11, November 18, and December 2.  It will occur at North Metro Community Services 1185 West 124th Avenue in Westminster.

The cost of the class is $300 per person.  Preregistration is required and must occur no later than October 25th.

Class size is limited to 30.

Understanding and Shaping the Behavior of Your Child with a Developmental Disability

Presented by The Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC



This class is designed for parents and other caregivers of children with developmental disabilities. 

The following will be taught in this class: 
•    How your actions, including well-meaning actions, can encourage problem behavior.
•    How to change your actions so that you don’t encourage problem behavior.
•    How situations in the environment can set up your child for problem behavior.
•    How to change these situations so that your child is less likely to use problem behavior.
•    How problem behavior is triggered.
•    How to reduce the number of triggers for problem behavior.
•    How to understand what your child is communicating through problem behavior.
•    How to respond to this communication so that you don’t encourage further problem behavior.

This class is scheduled to occur from 9:30 a.m. until 1:00 p.m. on October 24th and October 31st.  It will be held at the Daniels Fund, 101 Monroe Street, Denver, CO. 
The cost is $95 for those who pre-register and $120 at the door.
Class size is limited to 30.

A Parent’s Guide to Functional Behavior Assessment
Or
FBAs Made Understandable

Presented by The Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC

This class is for parents and others who are interested learning how functional behavior assessments (FBA) are conducted and how they are used to develop behavior change programs.
Schools and others often conduct FBAs to guide development of interventions for problem behavior.  An FBA can be conducted in many different ways.  This course essentially serves as a consumer’s guide to FBAs and will provide the participants with the following information.
•    What an FBA is.
•    How to determine if a child should have an FBA.
•    How to determine if an adequate FBA has been conducted.
•    How to determine if the results of the FBA are being used appropriately to guide intervention.

This class will occur from 9:30 a.m. until about 12:30 p.m. on November 3rd at at the Daniels Fund, 101 Monroe Street, Denver, CO.
The cost is $50 those who preregister and $65 for those who pay at the door.
Class size is limited to 30.
