Offered in Colorado Springs

Understanding and Shaping the Behavior of Your Child with a Developmental Disability

Presented by the Pikes Peak Chapter of the Autism Society of America and Towers Behavior Services LLC



This class is designed for parents and other caregivers of children with developmental disabilities. 

The following will be taught in this class: 
•    How your actions, including well-meaning actions, can encourage problem behavior.
•    How to change your actions so that you don’t encourage problem behavior.
•    How situations in the environment can set up your child for problem behavior.
•    How to change these situations so that your child is less likely to use problem behavior.
•    How problem behavior is triggered.
•    How to reduce the number of triggers for problem behavior.
•    How to understand what your child is communicating through problem behavior.
•    How to respond to this communication so that you don’t encourage further problem behavior.

This class is scheduled to occur from 1:30 p.m. until 4:00 p.m. on June 23 and June 30.  It will be held at the Resource Exchange, 418 South Weber Street, Colorado Springs, Colorado. 
The cost is $95 for those who pre-register and $120 at the door.
Class size is limited to 30.

Offered in Alamosa

Understanding and Shaping the Behavior of an Individual with a Developmental Disability

Presented by the Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC



The following will be taught in this class:

  • How your actions, including well-meaning actions, can encourage problem behavior.
  • How to change your actions so that you don’t encourage problem behavior.
  • How situations in the environment can set up an individual for problem behavior.
  • How to change these situations so that the individual is less likely to use problem behavior.
  • How problem behavior is triggered.
  • How to reduce the number of triggers for problem behavior.
  • How to understand what an individual is communicating through problem behavior.
  • How to respond to this communication so that you don’t encourage further problem behavior.
  • How to take direct observational data on an individual’s behavior.

This course is seven hours in length. The first half of this course will occur from 9:00 a.m. until 12:30 p.m. on July 24. The second half will occur from 9:00 a.m. until 12:30 p.m. on July 26. This class will occur at the San Luis Valley BOCES, 2261 Enterprise Drive, Alamosa, CO.

Class size is limited to 40.

The cost is $95 for those who pre-register and $120 at the door. It is likely that 1/2 semester hour of undergraduate or graduate credit will be available. Those wishing college credit will need to pay an additional $22.50 for tuition. Registration and payment for the credit will occur on the morning of the first day of the class.
