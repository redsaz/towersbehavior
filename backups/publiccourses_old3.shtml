Offered in Lakewood

Understanding and Shaping the Behavior of an Individual with a Developmental Disability

Presented by the Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC



The following will be taught in this class:

  • How your actions, including well-meaning actions, can encourage problem behavior.
  • How to change your actions so that you don't encourage problem behavior.
  • How situations in the environment can set up an individual for problem behavior.
  • How to change these situations so that the individual is less likely to use problem behavior.
  • How problem behavior is triggered.
  • How to reduce the number of triggers for problem behavior.
  • How to understand what an individual is communicating through problem behavior.
  • How to respond to this communication so that you don't encourage further problem behavior.

This course is seven hours in length. The first half of this course will occur from 9:30 a.m. until 1:00 p.m. on September 7th. The second half will occur from 9:30 a.m. until 1:00 p.m. on September 14th. The course will be held in the Autism Society of Colorado's training room, which is in the basement (level 1) of the First Bank building at 550 S. Wadsworth Blvd., Lakewood.

Class size is limited to 45.

The cost is $95 for those who pre-register and $120 at the door.

Evaluating Intervention Practices

Presented by the Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC



This training is intended to provide parents of children with autism and other care providers of individuals with autism with information and skills that will allow them to become more knowledgeable about interventions and therapies for individuals with autism. Topics to be covered in this training include:

  • The importance of goal setting.
  • Determining which interventions/therapies will help to meet the goals.
  • Determining if progress is being made toward the goals.
  • What current research says about some of the more common interventions/therapies.

This training is free to Autism Society of Colorado members and $15* to non-members (includes membership).

The course will be held will be held from 10:00 a.m. until noon on August 25th in the Autism Society of Colorado's training room, which is in the basement (level 1) of the First Bank building at 550 S. Wadsworth Blvd., Lakewood.

Space is limited to 45 participants. To reserve your spot, call the Autism Society of Colorado at 720-214-0794.

In-Home Interventions for Children with Autism

Presented by The Autism Society of Colorado and Towers Behavior Services LLC


This class is designed for parents and others who wish to provide in-home educational and behavioral interventions to children with autism.

Research has demonstrated that intensive intervention for children with autism yields very strong positive results throughout the child's entire lifetime. Parents and in-home services providers can be an essential part of making a dramatic, lifelong difference in the lives of children with autism. This course will provide the participants with the training necessary to provide these interventions under the guidance of a professional well skilled in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) interventions for children with autism.

This course will provide training in four areas:

  • The core characteristics of autism and related characteristics of autism.
  • Understanding why behavior occurs and what it is communicating.
  • Implementing behavior change programs.
  • Effective instruction techniques for teaching new skills to children with autism.

This class is scheduled to occur from 9:00 until 4:00 on November 3, November 10, November 17, December 8, and December 15, 2007, for a total of approximately 30 hours of instruction. It will occur in the Autism Society of Colorado's training room, which is in the basement (level 1) of the First Bank building at 550 S. Wadsworth Blvd., Lakewood, CO.

The cost of the class is $350 per person. Preregistration is required and must occur no later than October 29th, 2007.
